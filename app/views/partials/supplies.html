Get it Locally.

Sourcing locally can be time consuming and frustrating unless you live in a relatively urban area or an artsy college town. If you do, you may just be able to find everything you need in one stop.

To expedite your shopping trip, print out this page (or save a tree and pull it up on your phone) and have your local art supply store employee help you find everything you need.

I love Michael’s, JoAnn and Hobby Lobby, but they won’t have what you need. PROMISE. If you want to waste time and money, go ahead and get one of those yucky speedball calligraphy kits. If you want to enjoy your time with pen and ink, get the following:

Required Supplies:

  • Wooden or plastic pen holder
  • Black ink - FW, Higgins Eternal, Sumi 60
  • At least 2 Nibs - Hunt 22, Hunt 101, Gillott 303, Brause ef66, Leonardt Principal, Zebra G
  • Organizer box
  • Rhodia gridor Bienfang grid paper
  • Tracing paper
  • Pencil
  • White plastic eraser

Additional Supplies:

  • Oblique pen holder (strongly recommended for lefties)
  • Sumi 60 ink
  • Dr. PH Martin’s Pen White ink
  • Dr. PH Martin’s Copperplate Gold
  • All the pointed nibs you can find - Hunt 22, Hunt 101, Gillott 303, Brause ef66, Leonardt Principal, Zebra G
  • Drafting pencil
  • Crowquill holder
  • Click eraser
  • Clear gridded ruler

Nibs - you need a few kinds of nibs, the ones with the points on them. If they flatten out at the tip, you’re headed in the wrong direction. Here are some respectable nibs that are easy to find locally:

Nibs: Hunt 101, Hunt 100, Gillott 303 Harder to find: Zebra G, Brause ef66, Leonardt Principal

Holder: Straight wood holder with metal teeth Optional (lefties should get one): Speedball oblique holder

Ink: FW acrylic ink Optional: Sumi ink Stay away from Higgins!! Higgins Eternal is okay, but the others are AWFUL!

Paper: Gridded Rhodia pad, Tracing paper
